Co-housing

Co-housing communities offer a similar lifestyle to normal suburbs, but with less work. Here, the houses are closer in way that makes the yards smaller. This will mean less work for you and more time to spend with your friends and neighbors. These communities are for those who are 55+. They typically offer basic amenities and mainly focus on activities that help the community connect.




What is Senior Co-Housing?

Senior co-housing is a type of living community that combines private homes with clustered living spaces. A co-housing community typically consists to 20-40 single-family / attached homes where all of the homes share yards, walkways, and other communal areas like barbecue pits. Residents get their own personal living space in addition to a shared common house.


Why Choose Senior Co-Housing?

The most common reason people have for moving into a senior co-housing facility is to transition from a general co-housing community to one intended for the elderly. But that doesn't mean that someone has to have experience living in co-housing to want to move into a facility for seniors. Many people like the sense of community and increased social interaction among residents compared to normal neighborhoods. Since these senior co-housing facilities often share resources, residents often see economic and environmental benefits linked to reduced cost of living.


Where Can I Find Senior Co-Housing?

According to the Cohousing Association of the United State, there are 165 established communities throughout the nation, and of those, 13 are specifically for seniors only. Many more senior-only co-housing facilities are currently under construction.

Assisted Living

Assisted living facilities offer 24 hour assistance, although it's a bit less involved than what would be seen in a nursing home. These facilities do not have medical services such as on-call nurses or doctors. Services available for these type of facilities include laundry, social activities, assistance with bathing/toileting/dressing, medication assistance, security, and emergency calls.




What is Assisted Living?

Assisted living communities are a residential option for older adults who may need more care in their day-to-day lives than someone living in senior apartments or co-housing. Such care includes help cooking meals, bathroom assistance, housekeeping, and traveling, but does not include things commonly found in more involved care, such a round-the-clock medical care.

Other commonly used terms for these types of developments include:

  1. Residential care

  2. Adult care home

  3. Extra-care housing

  4. Alternative care facility

  5. Congregate care


Why Choose Assisted Living?

There are many reasons to choose assisted living over other forms of elderly housing. The most common reason is for the increased level of care over other communal living setups.

Some additional reasons include, but are not limited to:

  1. Feeling isolated or lonely at home / in need of social interaction

  2. Increased safety, especially for those who need help moving / walking

  3. Ease of maintenance

  4. Ease of transportation

  5. Easy access to organized recreational activities


Amenities at an Assisted Living Facility

Amenities typically include, but are not limited to:

  1. Three square meals a day

  2. Assistance with eating, bathing, dressing, walking, going to the bathroom

  3. Emergency call systems in each living space

  4. Medication management

  5. Social and recreational activities

  6. Exercise and wellness programs

  7. Security

  8. Housekeeping services

Assisted Living

Assisted living facilities offer 24 hour assistance, although it's a bit less involved than what would be seen in a nursing home. These facilities do not have medical services such as on-call nurses or doctors. Services available for these type of facilities include laundry, social activities, assistance with bathing/toileting/dressing, medication assistance, security, and emergency calls.




What is a 55+ Community?

A 55+ community is a residential development that puts a limit on the number of residents who are below the age of 55. Residents under the age of 55 typically take up less that 20 percent of the community. Most 55+ developments feature low-maintenance homes in addition to a variety of shared amenities, such as parks, swimming pools, activity centers, and more!

Other commonly used terms for these types of developments include:

  1. Age-qualified communities

  2. Age-restricted communities

  3. Active adult communities

  4. Planned retirement communities

  5. Lifestyle communities


Why Choose a 55+ Community?

According to a nation-wide survey, the biggest reasons for moving into a 55+ community are: to live closer to family; to get a home of higher quality; for social interaction with similarly-aged peers.

Some additional reasons include, but are not limited to:

  1. Fewer home-maintenance related responsibilities

  2. Homes with modern features

  3. Homes purposefully designed for aging in place

  4. More vibrant and active social lives

  5. Heightened safety and security

  6. Easy access to organized recreational activities
